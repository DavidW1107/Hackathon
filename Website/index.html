02 · How SONR works

Radar, rebuilt from sound.

Dozens of times per second, SONR runs the same loop a coastal radar station does — emit, listen, resolve — using nothing but the audio hardware already in the lid.

01 / EMIT

Inaudible chirps

Built-in speakers fire ultrasonic pulses far above human hearing. Silent to the room, loud and clear to the machine.

02 / LISTEN

Echo profiling

The microphone captures every returning echo. Each object in range — a chair, a wall, a person leaning in — leaves a distinct signature.

03 / RESOLVE

MTI subtraction

Moving-target indication subtracts the static room, aligns profiles, and range-gates to your space. What remains is a blip — and an alert.

04 · Questions

Asked before every rollout.

Can people hear the sweep?

No. The sweep sits at 40kHz — roughly twice the ceiling of human hearing. A pet-safe mode caps output power and duty cycle in spaces animals share.

Does SONR record conversations?

Never. SONR listens only for its own echoes inside a narrow ultrasonic band. Speech frequencies are filtered out in hardware DSP before anything reaches disk, and raw audio never leaves the device.

What hardware do I need?

Any laptop from the last decade. Built-in speakers and microphone are all SONR uses — the same machine you're reading this on is already a sonar station.

What if I type differently when I'm tired?

CADENCE keeps a rolling profile with confidence bands, so gradual drift is absorbed automatically. A genuinely off-rhythm attempt triggers step-up authentication — never a hard lockout.
